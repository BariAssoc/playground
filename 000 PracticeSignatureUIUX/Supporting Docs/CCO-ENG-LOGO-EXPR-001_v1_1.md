## §3. CLINICAL RATIONALE — THE HABIT FORMATION THESIS

A FAB becomes a biometric through bookends — warm-up before, content middle, cool-down after. Without bookends, content cannot be encoded as a habit by the body.

The same principle applies to **the delivery layer itself**. A notification without bookends — the model used by every other platform (Whoop, Oura, Apple Watch) — drops as a discrete event. The body experiences it as interruption, raises cortisol, and breaks routine.

**Rhythm Signal is the bookend that makes BioSnap a biometric.**

By the 90-day mark, the patient has internalized the logo's rhythm as proprioception. The Aurora Effect — the moment the patient *feels* the breath shift before consciously noticing the snap — is identity ignition.

This thesis is grounded in:
- The 466-patient cohort observation (2012–2015) — adherence improves with structured anchoring
- Bogdan / Professor Ilić's "constant presence" methodology
- The FAB-as-biometric architecture (CCO-FAB-001 v1.2)
- The "Live in Rithm" motto rendered literal — the logo IS the rhythm

## §4. CANONICAL DEFINITIONS

### 4.1 The Logo
The BariAccess logo, located in the top-right of the Rhythm Board header (per Voice Memory Rule, April 22, 2026). It is a **functional element, not decorative**. It holds Rhythm Signal and BioSnap as a unified expression organism.

### 4.2 Rhythm Signal
A continuous, ambient micro-pulse rendered on the logo. The breath of the system. Operates in three states: Normal · Attention · Snap Active. Fed primarily by **A6 (Trend Signals — 7/14/30/90-day slopes)** and pre-signal logic. Functions as the **warm-up bookend** before BioSnap delivery and the **cool-down trace** after.

### 4.3 BioSnap
An event-driven, ISE-governed snap that drops from the logo. The content / exhale. Seven canonical types (see §7.2). Fed by **A2 (Behavior Physiology) + clinical event triggers**. Functions as the **content middle** of the bookended sequence.

### 4.4 Bookended Biometric
A delivery sequence with a warm-up bookend before content and a cool-down trace after, allowing the body to encode the event as habit rather than interruption.

```

│   Rhythm Signal builds     BioSnap drops         COOL-DOWN │
│   Normal → Attention →     (one of 7 types)      Residual  │
│   Snap Active                                    trace     │
```

### 5.1 Phase 1 — Warm-up (Rhythm Signal)
The breath builds across the three states. Patient learns to feel it the way they learn to feel their own pulse — at first invisible, then unmissable. Can fire **with or without** a subsequent BioSnap (see §6.5).

### 5.2 Phase 2 — Content (BioSnap)
The exhale. The actual snap drops with payload. Routes through Ollie's Space first (locked Expression routing doctrine), then renders on the logo.

### 5.3 Phase 3 — Cool-down (Rhythm Signal Trace)
After acknowledgment, the logo does NOT snap back to baseline instantly. Returns to breath rhythm with a residual color trace and slightly altered cadence — duration varies by snap type (see §7.2). The patient learns: *"something just happened, the system is still aware."* Then fades to baseline.

**The cool-down trace is the bookend that closes the loop.** Without it, BioSnap is content without closure.

### 6.3 Cadence Per ISE State (PATCHED — v3.0 numbering + opacity spec)

**v1.0 used legacy ISE severity numbering (Optimal/Engaged/Drifting/Challenged/Protected/Vulnerable/Critical) which conflicted with ISE Canon v3.0.** This v1.1 patch re-keys the cadence table to ISE Canon v3.0 nomenclature (Neutral/Aligned/Protective/Contained/Momentum/Restricted/Exploratory) and adds the locked opacity gradient spec from OQ-LOGO-02.

**Cadence table (v3.0 numbering — LOCKED):**

| ISE State | Name | Cycle duration | Opacity range | Cadence character |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ISE-0** | Neutral / Baseline | 5–6 sec | 0.4 – 0.7 | Slow, steady — calm default presence |
| **ISE-1** | Aligned / Available | 4–5 sec | 0.5 – 0.8 | Slightly quicker, slightly brighter — active, alert |
| **ISE-2** | Protective | 6–7 sec | 0.3 – 0.6 | Slower, softer — recovery-forward |
| **ISE-3** | Contained | 6–8 sec | 0.3 – 0.5 | Slow, dim — reduce stimulation |
| **ISE-4** | Momentum | 5–6 sec | 0.5 – 0.8 | Steady reinforcement — no excessive lift |
| **ISE-5** | Restricted / Guarded | 8–10 sec | 0.2 – 0.4 | Near-static — hold space (clinical mode) |
| **ISE-6** | Exploratory / Low-Signal | 5–7 sec | 0.4 – 0.6 | Gentle, present — onboarding-friendly |
| **(reserved)** | Pearl White overnight | 4–6 sec | 0.3 – 0.6 | Already locked in CCO-ENG-EXPR-001 v2.0 §4.4 — guardian state |

**Cadence design rationale:**

Cycle durations follow **inverse-stimulation logic** — worse states (toward ISE-5) breathe slower to reduce cognitive load on a patient who already has reduced bandwidth. Better states (ISE-1) breathe slightly quicker to mirror engagement readiness.

Opacity ranges follow **attention-budget logic** — worse states render dimmer to avoid pulling attention from clinical priorities. ISE-5 sits near-static at 0.2–0.4 because in restricted/guarded states the system should hold space, not advertise itself.

ISE-0 (Neutral) and ISE-6 (Exploratory) share similar mid-range cadence because both represent low-information states — one is the default fallback, the other is the data-sparse onboarding state. Both should breathe present but unobtrusive.

### 6.3.1 Opacity Gradient Within Cycle (NEW — OQ-LOGO-02 LOCKED)

The Logo's breath operates with a single core tone per ISE state (the hue is the **state signal**) plus an opacity gradient within each breath cycle.

**Specification:**

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Hue per cycle** | ONE tone (constant during cycle) — drawn from the state's color authority per ISE permission map |
| **Opacity peak (inhale)** | Top of state range (e.g., ISE-0 = 0.7) |
| **Opacity trough (exhale)** | Bottom of state range (e.g., ISE-0 = 0.4) |
| **Opacity gradient amplitude** | ±10% of midpoint within the cycle |
| **Hue shift triggers** | ONLY on ISE state change OR cascade event. Never within a breath cycle. |

**Why opacity-only within cycle, not hue:**

A solid tone with no breath reads sterile and mechanical — wrong for the breath metaphor.
A full hue gradient (e.g., blue→green within a single breath) would conflict with the cascade color authority — the patient learns to read hue as state. If hue shifted within a breath, hue would lose its semantic clarity.
Opacity-only honors the organic breath metaphor while preserving hue as a stable state signal.

**Render frame implementation:**

| Mode | Frame rate |
|---|---|
| **Baseline breath** | 60fps render |
| **State transitions / cascade events** | 30fps for smooth interpolation between hues |

### 6.4 A6 Event Logging Specification (NEW — OQ-LOGO-04 LOCKED)

The Rhythm Signal is a continuous awareness training mechanism. Independent fires (where the breath builds toward Attention and returns to Normal without producing a BioSnap) are themselves canonical signals — they represent the system catching pre-pre-signal events that may not yet warrant patient interruption but are important for threshold calibration and Aurora Effect measurement.

**Logging principle:** State transitions are signal. Per-cycle breath events are noise. Log the former, skip the latter.

**Logged events:**

| Event | Logged? | Trigger condition |
|---|---|---|
| **Rhythm Signal state transition** (Normal ↔ Attention ↔ Snap Active) | ✅ YES | Every transition, both directions |
| **Independent fire return to baseline** (Attention → Normal without BioSnap drop) | ✅ YES | Critical pre-pre-signal event |
| **Per-cycle breath events** (4–6 sec inhale/exhale) | ❌ NO | Too noisy — not log-worthy |
| **BioSnap drop** | ✅ YES (already logged separately in BioSnap pipeline) | Once per drop |

**Log payload schema:**

```json
{
  "event_type": "rhythm_signal_transition",
  "timestamp": "ISO-8601 UTC",
  "patient_id": "<uuid>",
  "prior_state": "Normal | Attention | Snap Active",
  "new_state": "Normal | Attention | Snap Active",
  "ise_state_at_transition": "ISE-0 | ISE-1 | ISE-2 | ISE-3 | ISE-4 | ISE-5 | ISE-6",
  "a6_trigger_source": "<which trend slope caused the shift>",
  "resolved_with_biosnap": true | false,
  "biosnap_type": "<one of 7 types, if applicable>",
  "session_context": "<active | rest_window | sleep>"
}
```

**Storage routing:**

- Primary destination: **V2 (Behavior Physiology) stream** within ABAEMR
- Secondary consumer: **A6 boxes** for slope analysis and threshold calibration
- Aurora measurement consumer: feeds reaction-time analysis (per §18 Aurora Validation Plan)

**Rationale:**

Logging independent fires closes the feedback loop on threshold sensitivity. If patients consistently show Attention-state Rhythm Signals that return to Normal without subsequent BioSnaps, the thresholds may be too sensitive. Conversely, if patients reach Snap Active without prior Attention buildup, the Attention threshold may be too coarse. A6 needs this data to tune.

This logging is also the foundation for Aurora Effect measurement (OQ-LOGO-05 Phase 1) — reaction time to BioSnap drops can only be analyzed against the precise timestamp of each Rhythm Signal state transition leading up to the drop.

### 6.5 Independent Fire Rule *(was §6.4 in v1.0 — unchanged content, renumbered)*

Rhythm Signal **CAN fire without a subsequent BioSnap.** The breath may deepen toward Attention and return to Normal without ever reaching Snap Active. This is a feature, not a bug — it is the continuous awareness training mechanism that builds proprioception over 90 days.

**v1.1 cross-reference addition:** All independent fires are now logged per §6.4 above, and feed Aurora Effect measurement per §18.

### 7.2 Seven Canonical Types — PATCHED (cool-down duration added per type)

**v1.0 declared 7 BioSnap types but did not specify cool-down durations per type.** This v1.1 patch adds the cool-down duration as a property of each snap type, locked per OQ-LOGO-03.

**Cool-down design principle:** Cool-down trace duration is a property of the BioSnap type, not a runtime calculation. Spec-driven, deterministic. Different snap categories have different cognitive/biological integration windows, and cool-down matches the appropriate window.

**Seven canonical types with cool-down property:**

| Snap Type | Trigger Source | Cool-down Duration | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bio-Snap™** | HRV / sleep biometric alert — V1 data | **15 min** | Biological events deserve longer awareness window for body integration |
| **DriftSnap™** | FAB behavior drifting — A2 | **10 min (default)** | Behavioral drift — standard cool-down |
| **GoodSnap™** | Positive milestone — celebration / reinforcement | **5 min** | Reward absorbs quickly; longer trace would dilute positive impact |
| **OllieSnap™** | Ollie has a message — direct communication | **10 min (default)** | Communication — standard |
| **MemorySnap™** | Memory Lane related | **10 min (default)** | Memory event — standard |
| **TierSnap™** | Tier progression notification | **10 min (default)** | Tier progression — standard |
| **MerchantSnap™** | Merchant / redemption notification | **10 min (default)** | Marketplace event — standard |

**Special case — Clinical override (Red bypass):**
Clinical BioSnaps (those that fire as part of Red bypass per CCO-ENG-EXPR-001 v2.0 §4.5) do NOT use the standard cool-down trace. They route via the clinical pipeline; provider response governs the trace duration. Cool-down property: **N/A — provider-governed.**

**RETIRED:** ML-Snap™ (preserved from v1.0 retirement decision) — its function is now performed by the Rhythm Signal continuous breath layer.

## §9. HABIT FORMATION ARCHITECTURE — THE BOOKEND PRINCIPLE

### 9.1 The Three Bookends of the Logo

```
WARM-UP            CONTENT           COOL-DOWN
(Rhythm Signal)    (BioSnap)         (Rhythm Signal Trace)
       │                │                  │
       ▼                ▼                  ▼
  Anticipation    →  Delivery   →    Closure
   (build)         (release)         (residual)
```

### 9.2 Why This Matters Clinically
- **Without warm-up:** Notification is interruption. Cortisol rises. Routine breaks.
- **Without cool-down:** Notification is unfinished. Patient cannot encode it as a complete event.
- **With both:** Notification becomes a bookended biometric. Body encodes it as habit-class information.

### 9.3 The 90-Day Proprioception Curve
- **Days 1–7:** Patient does not consciously notice the breath
- **Days 7–30:** Patient begins to recognize Attention state
- **Days 30–90:** Patient anticipates Snap Active before it fires
- **Day 90+:** The Aurora Effect — patient feels the system before the system speaks

This is the same architecture that makes the 90-day Bandit prior (RITHM Bandit Engine) so valuable — by Day 90, the system AND the patient have internalized each other's rhythm.

## §18. AURORA VALIDATION PLAN (NEW IN v1.1)

### 18.1 Purpose

The Aurora Effect is the moment a patient *feels* the breath of the Logo shift before consciously noticing the BioSnap drop. It is identity ignition — the proof point at Day 90+ that the patient and the system have internalized each other's rhythm.

This plan operationalizes the measurement of the Aurora Effect using a three-phase approach. Each phase uses progressively higher-fidelity instrumentation, allowing early-stage measurement to begin in Phase 1 build while research-grade validation accrues over time.

### 18.2 Why Measurement Matters

Three reasons:

1. **Validation of clinical thesis.** The Habit Hypothesis (Expression Canon §5 Rule 3 + Logo Canon §3) asserts that bookended delivery encodes habit information into the body. Aurora measurement is one of the validation pathways for that claim.

2. **Engagement metric for investor/research narrative.** A measurable proprioception curve from Day 1 to Day 90 is a unique platform-specific outcome no other digital health platform can claim. This becomes part of the BariAccess validation portfolio (research lead: Lisa).

3. **Threshold tuning feedback loop.** Patient anticipation patterns inform A6 threshold sensitivity calibration. If most patients anticipate by Day 60, thresholds may be too predictable; if anticipation never builds, thresholds may be too erratic.

### 18.3 Phase 1 — Behavioral Proxy (Phase 1 Build)

**Implementation effort:** Easy — leverages existing app interaction logs.

**Mechanism:** Compare patient's BioSnap reaction time across the 90-day window.

**Metric:** `reaction_time_delta`

```
reaction_time_delta = mean(reaction_times_days_60_to_90)
                    - mean(reaction_times_days_0_to_30)
```

**Interpretation:**

| Delta value | Reading |
|---|---|
| Significantly negative (faster) | Patient is anticipating — Aurora Effect building |
| Near zero | Neutral — no Aurora signal yet |
| Significantly positive (slower) | Patient may be disengaging — flag for Pamela / provider review |

**Data dependencies:**

- BioSnap drop timestamps (already captured)
- Patient interaction timestamps (already captured)
- Rhythm Signal state transition logs (newly required per §6.4)

**Deliverable:** A nightly batch job computing reaction_time_delta per active patient. Surface result on Provider Dashboard at Day 30, 60, 90 milestones.

### 18.4 Phase 2 — Self-Report (Post-Phase 1)

**Implementation effort:** Easy — integrates into Pamela's BBS workflow.

**Mechanism:** Periodic surveys at Day 30, 60, 90, and 180.

**Survey question (canonical wording):**

> *"In the last week, did you notice anything happening on the logo just before a notification dropped?"*

**Response options:**

| Response | Score |
|---|---|
| "Yes, almost every time" | 1.0 |
| "Yes, sometimes" | 0.7 |
| "Maybe — I'm not sure" | 0.4 |
| "No, never" | 0.0 |
| "I don't notice the logo" | 0.0 |

**Open-ended follow-up (optional):** *"Can you describe what you noticed?"*

**Survey delivery:**

- Embedded in BBS check-in workflow at Day 30, 60, 90, 180
- Pamela administers as part of regular touchpoint — not as standalone push notification
- Data flows to V2 stream and Aurora index calculation

**Cross-reference:** Self-report responses combined with Phase 1 behavioral proxy produces a more robust Aurora signal than either source alone.

### 18.5 Phase 3 — Wearable HRV Correlation (Post-90-Day Cohort)

**Implementation effort:** Research-grade — requires HRV data at exact BioSnap drop timestamp.

**Mechanism:** Compare HRV reactivity at the moment a BioSnap drops, across the 90-day window.

**Hypothesis:**

> *Patients who anticipate BioSnap drops (high Aurora) will show reduced cortisol/HRV spike at the moment of drop, because the body has already prepared for the event. Patients who do not anticipate will show classical interruption-pattern HRV reactivity.*

**Metric:** `hrv_reactivity_attenuation`

```
hrv_reactivity_attenuation = HRV_reactivity_days_0_to_30
                            - HRV_reactivity_days_60_to_90
```

**Data dependencies:**

- Continuous wearable HRV stream (currently Polar 360 / Oura Gen 4 / Garmin Venu 4 / WHOOP 5.0 per locked vendor list)
- Precise BioSnap drop timestamps (already captured)
- Patient consent for granular HRV correlation analysis (additional consent layer; coordinate with HIPAA workflow)

**Cross-reference:** Phase 3 enters the BariAccess research validation portfolio — flagged for inclusion in any IRB exemption pathway prepared by Stan.

### 18.6 Composite KPI — `aurora_anticipation_index`

The three phases combine into a single composite KPI.

**Range:** 0.0 – 1.0

**Formula (proposed — refinement open):**

```
aurora_anticipation_index = 0.40 × normalized_reaction_time_delta
                          + 0.35 × self_report_score
                          + 0.25 × hrv_reactivity_attenuation
```

Where:
- `normalized_reaction_time_delta` is rescaled to 0.0–1.0 (faster reactions → higher score)
- `self_report_score` is the 0.0–1.0 score from §18.4
- `hrv_reactivity_attenuation` is rescaled to 0.0–1.0 (more attenuation → higher score)

**Weighting rationale:**

- Reaction time (0.40) gets the highest weight because it's the most objective and most continuously available metric across the cohort
- Self-report (0.35) carries strong weight because direct experience of the Aurora Effect is the canonical marker — but it's subject to reporting bias
- HRV correlation (0.25) is research-grade gold-standard but data-sparse and consent-gated, so weight reflects availability constraints

**Note:** Final weighting requires validation against actual cohort data. Initial weights are proposed; refinement is expected during Phase 1 deployment and Phase 2 self-report data accrual.
